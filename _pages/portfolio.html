This page summarizes selected research and engineering projects in biomedical AI and scientific imaging. The focus is on robust modeling, interpretability, and deployment-oriented outcomes.

1) Self-supervised Learning for Multi-channel Brain Imaging

I developed a hierarchical self-supervised framework for high-dimensional spatial imaging data (15 channels), enabling representation learning without dense labels. The method improves topology-aware characterization of brain tissue regions and supports downstream disease-related analysis.

2) Large Vision-Language Modeling for Spatial Proteomics

I built a domain-adapted vision-language workflow for brain disease studies (including injury and neurodegeneration). The pipeline combines LoRA-based fine-tuning and retrieval-augmented context to increase response quality and interpretability on complex biomedical imagery.

3) Weak-to-Strong Cell Segmentation for Dense Overlaps

I designed a weak-to-strong learning strategy that transfers generalizable priors from foundation models into specialized cell segmentation. This improves boundary recovery and instance separation in highly crowded microscopy scenes.

4) High-throughput Catalyst Screening with Graph Neural Networks

I developed a machine learning workflow for catalyst screening, using graph-based representations to capture structural relationships and improve prediction quality for high-throughput candidate evaluation.

5) Video Interpolation with Diffusion Models

Ongoing work on diffusion-based temporal interpolation for scientific video data, targeting motion continuity and artifact reduction in challenging recording conditions.

6) Underwater Image Deblurring and Efficient Deployment

I implemented GAN-based deblurring for underwater imagery and explored model compression/quantization to support practical deployment on robotic platforms.
